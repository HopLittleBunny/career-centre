# Evidence safety

## Evidence record

Every reusable claim has:

- Stable evidence ID.
- Claim text and safe wording.
- Source type and source name.
- Exact supporting excerpt or user confirmation note.
- Confidence: `source_only`, `user_confirmed`, or `externally_corroborated`.
- Restrictions and unsafe extrapolations.
- Relevant role families.
- Date captured or confirmed.

## Source types

- `current_cv`
- `historical_cv`
- `user_confirmed`
- `uploaded_evidence`
- `external_source`

A claim extracted from a CV starts as `source_only`, even when written confidently in the CV. Never label it independently verified without corroborating evidence.

## Claim rules

- Preserve exact metrics, scope and ownership.
- Do not convert exposure into expertise.
- Do not convert participation into leadership.
- Do not add tools because they appear in a job description.
- Do not infer citizenship, clearance, licences or work rights.
- Do not imply a product is launched when the evidence says beta, prototype or being built.
- When wording is ambiguous, weaken it or ask the user.

## Tailored documents

Every summary sentence, role-match bullet, experience bullet, qualification and material cover-letter proof needs at least one evidence ID. Skills may be grouped only when each skill is supported by evidence.

Future-oriented contribution paragraphs may be evidence-free only when clearly framed as a proposed approach rather than a past achievement.
